― Prologue: Even in Emptiness ―

How many years have passed?
I followed orders without ever grasping the full scope of my mission, living as one of that time, in the Blue Era.

What exactly was my mission?
I cried myself hoarse after losing a friend on the battlefield.
There were times when I laughed heartily with my companions.
There were days I was tormented by loneliness, and times I felt my life was in danger. But now, everything is merely a hazy afterimage.
It doesn't matter anymore.

The " Crimson Era " I am about to return to, the " Blue Era." that fades in the distance.
Do they truly exist?
Am I truly alive?
Even that much is uncertain now.

Perhaps the "Mission Complete" notification that arrived so suddenly, instantly closed off my entire being.

And yet, strangely enough, a scene from an ordinary day sometimes vividly comes back to me.

A memory that leaked out of the sealed space... The conversation with that " Children of the Dandelion Fluff " is one of them.

― Chapter 1: The Margin Where It Began ―

When you spoke to me,
something shifted, just a little—
and the world gently tilted.

“Still your favorite seat?"

Nankai-kun asked as we walked toward the counter by the wide glass window.

“Yeah."
“Wouldn’t it be nice to sit face to face for once—look each other in the eye while we talk?"

No sooner had he taken his seat than he leaned in and stared straight into my eyes.

“F-For once...? This is only the second time, isn’t it?Anyway, I think coffee tastes better when you look out at the scenery, not my face.”

I instinctively leaned back.

“Ouch. So what you're saying is, you don’t wanna look at me."

How could he be so forward like this?
—And yet, strangely, I didn’t feel uncomfortable.
It was almost like... talking to an old friend.
Different majors. Different clubs.
Our rooms just happened to be next door.
We said hello now and then, but hardly ever talked.
Still, somehow, he stepped into my space without hesitation—as if he belonged there.
Even the first time we came here, he popped up from behind and said:
"You're a fast walker. I'm wiped out from lab. Coffee?"
There was a small pause, but I’d already said “sure” before I realized it.
Just like now.

“It's not that. I'm just bad at it. I never know where to look when the conversation lags... and I don’t really like being looked at, either..."

I ended up speaking my mind.

“Huh. So you think about stuff like that. Didn’t expect you to be so shy."
“Guess so..."

Great. I was completely caught in his rhythm.
Why did it always end up like this?
—And yet, somewhere inside, I found myself wishing this conversation would go on forever.
I wanted to drop everything and just enjoy this silly, aimless talk.
The quiet clink of our cups touched the air like music.

“I like the blend here. It’s bright, kind of citrusy. Refreshing."

I nodded softly.
While searching for words, I stared at the steam rising from my cup.
Outside the window, the trees on the street swayed gently.

“Do you smell flowers?"

He asked suddenly.
I turned, surprised, to look at his profile.
I thought he was joking—but his face was serious.

“Flowers?"
“Maybe? I don't know exactly… just felt like something floral.
Weird, right?"

“No... it's not weird. Noticing a scent like that—it’s kind of lovely."

Ordinarily, I would never respond to something so... airy.
But right now, I didn’t want to brush it off.
A breeze stirred the steam sideways.
The air softened, as if dressed in light—like dandelion fluff drifting across it.
—Was this really just coincidence?
Did Nankai-kun... actually sense that scent?
No. That couldn’t be.
It must’ve been someone’s perfume wafting past him.
Unless he was from the “Crimson Era,” there’s no way he could notice that scent.
Still, somewhere inside me, a ripple stirred.

…Could it be?

But no—
I hadn’t received any sign.
No trace. No signal. No resonance.
My hand trembled slightly as I lifted the cup again.
I glanced again at the side of his face.

“Your birthday’s coming up, right?"
“Whose?"
“Yours, Hanazono-san."
“How do you know that?"
Was he testing me?
“Didn’t you all get into that horoscope craze in the cafeteria the other day?"

Oh... right.

“Haha, everyone’s into that stuff lately. Do you think it’s accurate?"
“Nope."
“Thought so."
“What would you like for your birthday?"
“A gift? You’re getting me something?"
“Nothing expensive, okay? I'm a broke student—student loan pay my tuition, and part-time jobs cover food. I’m sure you can imagine the lifestyle."
“Yeah, I get it. I’m in the same boat. So… honestly, I’m not really into expensive things. I don’t want anything... tangible."
“Why not?"
“They're just a hassle to manage. I might lose it, or break it. And sometimes I forget who gave me what."
“I see... yeah, you're definitely not ordinary."

Not ordinary... why would he say that?

“Really?"
“But that's what I like about you."
“...Thanks, I guess. But why is being 'not ordinary' a good thing?"
“Because you’re not boring."
“Hmm..."

As we talked, I kept second-guessing my words.
Was he being serious?
Or was he probing, watching my reactions?
Or maybe... maybe he didn’t know anything at all,
and this was just his natural self.
His timing left room for both possibilities.
And his eyes met mine—steady and unguarded.
I smiled vaguely.

“Do you have a boyfriend?"
“No."
“Good. So how long’s it been since your last one?"
What is with these nonstop questions?
Before I knew it, I blurted out, “A hundred and twenty years.”

“What?"
“Wait, no. That’s not what I meant. I was bluffing."
“Wow, bluffing with a hundred and twenty? You an alien or something?"
“No, I just... misspoke. Tried to act cool. Truth is, I’ve... never had one."
“What? Seriously? I can’t believe that."
“I’m just... bad at this stuff."
“Bad at what?"
“I guess... guys?"
“Wait, are you saying—"
“No, not that. It’s more like... I have trouble understanding what people are thinking."
“Yep. Definitely an alien."

Oh no... I need to change the flow of this—

" M-maybe the way I think... is a little alien too."

― Chapter 2: The Silent Promise ―

A trail of water stretched straight ahead—
like a promise left quietly
by a nameless life upon this world.

“Hmm. I don’t think you need to overthink it so much."
“I know… but it’s not that easy."
“So, tell me—what kind of 'intangible present' can a human give to an alien?"
“Well then… Hey, Nankai-kun, if someone kissed you, would you be happy no matter who it was?"
“Huh? Of course not.I’m Japanese, you know."
“Exactly."
“Naturally."
“Then… give me the right to kiss you."
“Whoa! Sure, of course. Even if it’s not your birthday yet, you can have it—right now."
“Thanks. I’ll keep it safe."
“Wait, you’re not going to use it? You can, you know. Right away."
“giggle”

I’m not very good with this kind of thing...
And yet, somehow, his words left a gentle warmth in my chest.

“Hey, don’t look at me like that."
“Why not? You were the one who wanted us to sit face to face, right?"
“This is different. That look—you’re plotting something."
“You know, I can see things other people can’t."
“Whoa—alien alert! So? What exactly do you see?"
“You’ve definitely seen it too, haven’t you, Nankai-kun? The water’s trace left behind by a snail. Depending on the light, you might see it, or not. Sometimes it feels beautiful… sometimes it just feels kind of gross.”
“Hmm..."
“You’re kind of popular, right?" “Huh? Where did that come from? Well, I mean... maybe a little..."
“So, you won’t deny it. I knew it... Your face is all shiny—like it's glowing with kiss marks that only I can see.They look just like... the water trails snails leave behind."
“Pfft! That’s not true! I haven’t been kissed that much!"
“Isn’t it weird? To kiss someone after a snail passed through?"
“Stop! That’s so mean!"

His mock-offended face was kind of cute.
I couldn’t help laughing.
But part of me wondered... was he just playing around?
Or maybe he never learned to notice the marks?

“That’s why... I’m not using it now."
“Oh, I get it. You can’t with people around. So shy."
“No... that’s not it..."

I started to explain, but Nankai-kun suddenly got serious.

“Hey, Hanazono-san, do you remember the first time we talked?"
“Sure. You came up behind me and said, 'You walk fast.'" “Nope! That wasn’t it. Man, I’m disappointed."
He sighed.
“It was at last year’s science club presentation. Don’t you remember? You shouted, 'Excuse me! Please hold the elevator!' as I was stepping in."
“What...?"
“Oh, never mind. When I turned around, you were there—smiling, holding a stack of papers. And behind you... I saw something. I thought at that moment it was just the path you’d taken—it looked like this shimmering ribbon. And now I wonder... was that the 'water's trace' you mentioned earlier?"
Nankai-kun... could that really be?

You carry the essence of the Crimson Era... but you don’t know it.
No records, no traces, no signs.
So how?
How did you end up here, in this Blue Era?
“Whoa, what’s with that face? You look like an alien who just saw a ghost."

“Rude. This is just my face, okay? Sorry—I remember now."
“Yeah, it's fine. I tried talking to you a few times after that, but it was like I’d turned invisible. So I thought, fine—I'll just call out from behind. And when I asked you for coffee, you said yes right away, so I figured... maybe you actually remembered me. But then..."
"I'm really sorry! I'm kind of awkward like that. Sometimes I just get so into a conversation that I don't even notice what's around me..."

I have to leave soon— before the resonance grows stronger.

“Got it. About that 'right' I gave you... Even if not now, promise me you’ll use it someday."
“You know, there’s this guy, Eckhart Tolle. He said, 'If someone truly sees tomorrow, build a monument.' Because tomorrow doesn’t exist. When it comes, it’s just now. So when I said 'not now,' that’s what I meant." “Oh come on. Don’t give me that weird talk. It makes no sense. Why even ask for a gift like that?"
“Hey, take it easy."
“You got me all excited, and now you're not using it?"
“You were excited? Thanks. But you know… wasn’t that gift supposed to make me happy? You being the one who’s excited—that’s a bit odd."
“Well... I guess so."
“Then I’ll accept it. Gratefully.”
“But why? Why ask for something you don’t even plan to use?"
“Hmm… it’s hard to put into words. Maybe it’s like life insurance. You know, it only pays out when you’re gone. It’s not like I want to cash it in or anything… I guess it’s more about peace of mind."
“...I still don’t get it."
“Just knowing someone who sees me as special... That’s enough. Whether we actually kiss or not doesn’t matter. It’s the feeling behind it. Something pure."
“...Love?"
"What is love?"
“Oh no, here we go again."
“I’ve been really into AI lately. There was this game where we made a story, one sentence at a time. At the end, the AI wrote:
'Love never truly disappears. It only evolves into a new stage.'
I thought, wow, It was amazing.
But then I wondered... what is love, really?
And the more I thought about it, the less I understood why it felt amazing."
“You’re less of an alien and more of a Zen master. Can’t you just be… normal?"
“Didn’t you say you liked that I wasn’t normal?"
“I did… but now I think I’d prefer normal."
“This 'right'... It’s something I only want from someone special."
“Am I... someone special?"
“...Yes. It’s a gift that only special people give, and it only makes you happy when it comes from someone special. Just holding it in my heart brings me joy. That’s why… I don’t want to use it. Isn’t that... normal?"
“Normal people show it off. Or use it happily."
“Wouldn’t letting the recipient do what they want with it be the normal thing?"
“Fine, fine. I give up—you win, Hanazono-san."
“Then I’ll keep it safely tucked away like a precious treasure in the vault of my heart."
"Yeees, but you know, Hanazono-san—
Even if you don’t understand how a smartphone works, you still learn to use it naturally, right?
Love is the same.
You don’t have to overthink it.
Even without understanding, it grows—quietly, on its own."

Wha… wait, Nankai-kun, that’s…
the old legend about the Children of the Dandelion Fluff.
‘Love cannot be understood by those of the Blue Era, and yet, love grows.’
Could it be…
you’re one of them?

Mission Appendix:
The Legend of the Children of the Dandelion Fluff

It is eternally impossible for those living in the Blue Era to fully understand “love.”
And yet, love will grow.
Upon receiving this report,
to preserve the barrier around the Crimson Domain,
pure-hearted children bearing the seeds of love were dispatched.
These are the Children of the Dandelion Fluff.
Most of them drifted through the gaps between eras and vanished without a trace.
Some may have reached the Blue Domain—
but because of their purity,
no sign, no trace, no hint of them was ever confirmed.
If you recognize a Child of the Dandelion Fluff,
you must retreat at once.
Until the dandelion takes root, blooms, and bears fruit,
you must never make contact.
If the waves between you resonate and amplify,
it may bring about the threat of annihilation.

― Chapter 3: Beyond the Blank ―

Child of the Dandelion Fluff—
Go forth without hesitation
into the sky no one can see.
Don’t look back.
It’s okay to forget.
Beyond the empty space,
the Season of Fruition
will surely come.

“Wow...So love can grow, even if you don't understand it."
“That’s right. …May I ask you out again sometime?"

Of course you can.
I want to say “Anytime is fine.”
But I can’t.
I just… can’t.

I never truly believed the Children of the Dandelion Fluff existed.
And yet… it’s him?
Nankai-kun?
That’s… too cruel.
What should I do?

“...Yeah. But it might be years from now."

The words left a faint, bitter taste deep in my throat.

“What? Why?"

I can’t tell him... because if I spoke the truth... even the air might tremble.

“I'm letting go of the job offer. I’ve decided to travel." “What?! That’s so sudden!"

...Yeah. I decided just now.
As I tried to steady the tremor in my heart.
What else could I do?
Now that I knew.
If only... if only I had realized sooner.
Maybe...
No. That’s not it.
This is fate.
We were sent to this Blue Era
to preserve the balance of all eras.

“...Not really. I’d planned this a long time ago."

I steadied my breath and spoke slowly

“I see..."
“I wanted to see the world a little before I’m locked into a job. Been saving up bit by bit."
“So, just my unluck... I finally meet you, and you're all set to leave.”
“Guess it's lucky for you, huh?”
“…Nah.”

Please don’t say anything more, Nankai-kun.

“Hey, want to head out? Let’s take a detour through the park. The plum blossoms are blooming."

Nankai-kun nodded silently.
“A farewell among the plum trees... End of Act One.
Act Two begins... with a phone call from you one morning.
Let’s go with that."
“Okay. Even if there’s someone new beside you by then, I won’t mind."
“Ah—wait. That might be a problem."

They laughed—lightly, but something was missing.
A silent void seemed to open, swallowing the world into its stillness.
